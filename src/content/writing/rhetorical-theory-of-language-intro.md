What would it mean to begin with language not as something we *use* but as something we are used *by*? This series takes up that question across several short essays — on affirmation and negation, on Heidegger and language, on the line between linguistics and rhetoric, and on metaphor (not *that* metaphor).

The wager is simple to state and hard to hold onto: that rhetoric is not a layer added on top of meaning but the very condition under which meaning happens at all.
